Resources on Childhood Trauma

Evidence-based tools for children, teens, caregivers, and professionals

üìñ What is Childhood Trauma?

Childhood trauma refers to experiences that overwhelm a child's ability to cope‚Äîincluding abuse, neglect, loss of a caregiver, witnessing violence, or natural disasters. Trauma affects brain development, particularly the amygdala (fear response), hippocampus (memory), and prefrontal cortex (decision-making).

The good news: With evidence-based support like TF-CBT (Trauma-Focused Cognitive Behavioral Therapy), co-regulation with trusted adults, and skills like grounding techniques, children's brains can heal and build resilience.

Below are free, curated resources organized by skill area to support trauma recovery in children ages 3-18.
