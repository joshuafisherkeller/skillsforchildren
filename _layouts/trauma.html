What is TF-CBT?

Trauma-Focused Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (TF-CBT) is an evidence-informed treatment that helps children ages 3–18 and their caregivers overcome the effects of trauma. Developed by Cohen, Mannarino, and Deblinger, TF-CBT uses the PRACTICE framework to build coping skills, process trauma memories, and restore safe family relationships.

The framework

All 8 PRACTICE components

TF-CBT is organized around 8 skill-building components, delivered to child and caregiver in parallel. Each component below links to a companion song and, where applicable, resources in the library below.

P

Psychoeducation

Teaching children and caregivers about trauma, how it affects the brain and body, and why their reactions make sense.

🎵 Brain Alarm
R

Relaxation

Building a toolkit of calming skills — deep breathing, progressive muscle relaxation, and body-based techniques that work in and out of session.

🎵 Breathe Like Waves
A

Affective Modulation

Identifying, labeling, and managing difficult emotions — helping children move from "I feel bad" to "I feel scared, and I know what to do."

🎵 Name It, Choose It
C

Cognitive Coping

Recognizing the connections between thoughts, feelings, and behaviors — and learning to challenge unhelpful thinking patterns about the trauma.

🎵 Maybe, Maybe Not
T

Trauma Narrative

Gradually telling the story of what happened in a safe, supported way — reducing the emotional charge of trauma memories over time.

🎵 My Story, My Voice
I

In Vivo Mastery

Facing avoided situations or trauma reminders in a gradual, supported way — building confidence and reducing avoidance over time.

🎵 One Step Braver
C

Conjoint Sessions

Bringing the child and caregiver together to share parts of the trauma narrative, strengthen communication, and restore the caregiving relationship.

🎵 Say It With Me
E

Enhancing Safety

Teaching personal safety skills — knowing trusted people, recognizing unsafe situations, and knowing what to do and who to call.

🎵 My Plan, My People

What is childhood trauma?

Childhood trauma refers to experiences that overwhelm a child’s ability to cope — abuse, neglect, loss of a caregiver, witnessing violence, or natural disasters. Trauma affects brain development, particularly the amygdala, hippocampus, and prefrontal cortex.

The good news: with evidence-informed support like TF-CBT, co-regulation with trusted adults, and grounding techniques, children’s brains can heal and build resilience. Below are free, curated resources organized by skill area for ages 3–18.

FAQ

Common questions about TF-CBT

What does TF-CBT stand for? ▼
Trauma-Focused Cognitive Behavioral Therapy. It’s a structured, short-term treatment (typically 12–25 sessions) that helps children and their caregivers process trauma and build coping skills together.
Who is TF-CBT for? ▼
TF-CBT is designed for children and adolescents ages 3–18 who have experienced trauma — including abuse, neglect, witnessing violence, loss of a caregiver, accidents, or natural disasters. It is delivered by a trained mental health professional and always involves a caregiver.
How long does TF-CBT take? ▼
Most TF-CBT treatment courses run 12–25 sessions, typically once a week. The timeline depends on the number and severity of traumas, the child’s age and developmental level, and caregiver involvement. Many children see significant improvement within 16 sessions.
Can parents be involved in TF-CBT? ▼
Yes — caregiver involvement is a core part of TF-CBT, not optional. Caregivers participate in their own parallel sessions learning the same skills, and are brought together with the child in conjoint sessions near the end of treatment. Research consistently shows that caregiver involvement improves outcomes.
